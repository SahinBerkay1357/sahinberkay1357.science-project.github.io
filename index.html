Lab Safety

Introduction to Lab Safety

Lab safety is an important thing to know when in the lab. Without proper handling, severe injuries are highly possible. This is why it is important to learn proper procedures for lab safety. These rules are outlined on Flinn Scientific's website.

Looking through those rules, it might be overwhelming. I mean, 55 rules! That's a lot of rules. Well thankfully, most of these rules boil down to the same thing: Just don't be stupid! As long as you are using your brain and not running around like a crazy person, you should be perfectly fine. To show how simple it is to stay safe in the lab, I'll give you examples of what to do, and what not to do.

Examples

Rule 27

Report any accident (spill, breakage, etc.) or injury (cut, burn, etc.) to the instructor immediately, no matter how trivial it may appear.

What Happened?

Imagine you were working on a lab involving some chemicals. You turned around to see what your friend was saying and you happened to knock over the glass beaker full of the chemical over. What do you do?

What do Definitely not do

Thinking that the professor would be mad at you for spilling the chemical, you decide to just get some paper towels and wipe it up. What's the worst that can happen, right?

What to do

Realizing your mistake, you quickly tell the professor that you spilled the chemical. They call in some cleaners and they clean it properly and safely. You are happy that you didn't get hurt trying to handle the chemical and the professor is glad you told them what happened.

Rule 40

Carry glass tubing, especially long pieces, in a vertical position to minimize the likelihood of breakage and injury.

What Happened?

Imagine you were tasked with carrying a glass tube filled with a chemical you need for a lab. You have to take it from one table to another. What do you do?

What to Definitely not do

It's only a couple feet away, so you decide to carry it sideways. It's not like it's gonna fall over, is it?

What to do

Even though the tables are only a couple feet apart, you know that you should carry the tube upright to stay safe. You successfully get the tube from one table to another and you never had to worry about it falling.

Rule 48

Do not immerse hot glassware in cold water; it may shatter.

What Happened?

You just finished a lab that involved heating up chemicals in glassware. Now it's time to clean up. What do you do?

What to Definitely not do

Since you need to clean out the glassware, you decide to wash it under some cold water, without even allowing time for it to cool down. It's not like the glass is gonna shatter, right?

What to do

Since you know that hot glassware should not be put under cold water, you decide to ask the professor how you should clean up. With their help, you clean up after yourself and avoid getting hurt.
